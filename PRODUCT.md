# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two distinct roles sharing the same platform:

- **Doctoral students**: re-enrolling each academic year. They are time-constrained, often anxious about deadlines and document requirements, and expect the process to feel less painful than the legacy systems they've used before. Primary task: submit a re-enrollment request, upload required documents, track status.
- **Administrative staff**: reviewing, validating, or rejecting requests. They process many files and need density and speed. Primary task: triage incoming requests, verify compliance, move cases forward.

Both groups are educated adults who tolerate no ambiguity. Neither group should feel like they're operating the wrong side of the interface.

## Product Purpose

A two-sided platform for managing doctoral re-enrollment at a French university. Students initiate and track re-enrollment requests; administrative staff process and validate them. The product replaces or supplements legacy portals (ENT, Apogée) that are functional but hostile.

Success looks like: a student completes re-enrollment in one session without calling the secretary, and an admin can process a day's queue without copy-pasting between tabs.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, Clear, Efficient.

Voice: direct and precise. No filler phrases. Institutional enough to carry authority; modern enough to not feel abandoned. French-language interface tone: formal but not cold ("Votre dossier a été transmis" not "Félicitations, vous avez soumis votre demande!").

## Anti-references

- Legacy university portals: ENT, Apogée, ADE. Cluttered, inconsistent, technically hostile.
- Generic government portal aesthetic: flat primary blue, Comic Sans-era iconography, table-heavy layouts with no hierarchy.
- SaaS-cream template look: off-white backgrounds, rounded pill buttons, Inter font, identical card grids. Competent but characterless.

## Design Principles

1. **Institutional weight without institutional baggage.** This is a credible official platform, not a startup. But credibility comes from clarity and consistency, not from heavy blues and government-icon clip art.
2. **Two-audience clarity.** Students and admins have fundamentally different needs. The interface should never make either role feel like a secondary user.
3. **Process transparency.** Re-enrollment has steps, deadlines, and document requirements. The current state and next action must always be visible. No dead ends, no silent failures.
4. **Efficiency as respect.** Every unnecessary click, every confirmation dialog, every redundant form field is a failure. The platform respects the user's time as a given.
5. **Trust through predictability.** Every repeated action should behave identically. No surprises. The system should feel like it knows what it's doing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Assume users on a range of devices (students on phones, admins on desktop). Reduced-motion support should be built in from the start. Color contrast must be verified, not assumed.
